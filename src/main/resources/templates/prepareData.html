Prepare Your Data


There are many ways to connect to data from within Tableau, including external databases, PDF files, Excel spreadsheets, and "flat files" like csv (comma separated value) files that might hold data prepared using Python or R.

Tips:


  • Where possible, before bringing your data into Tableau, assemble your data into one file, clean up formatting and spelling errors, and delete unnecessary information.

  • If you use Excel to prepare your data:
    • Eliminate spreadsheet titles, subheadings, footnotes, remarks, and comments from the body of the spreadsheet.
    • Footnotes or comments can be added as an additional column to the spreadsheet IF they relate to particular lines and are given a column title.
    • Delete subtotals and totals.
    • Make sure that each column's name and its data align properly.
  • Tableau will read the first line of your data as column names and each subsequent row as a row of data. Therefore:
    • DO NOT include totals in your data.
    • DO NOT put your data in a matrix.
    • DO NOT include an extra heading over a group of columns.

  • Tableau will mis-read your data if you do any of those.

  • You can use Tableau Prep to assist in preparing your data, but it still is best to do as much as possible outside Tableau Prep before using it.

So I Did All That. Now What?


When you open Tableau Desktop, in the left side panel, you can Connect To a File or Connect to a Server. Once you have done so, click on the Data Source tab in the bottom left corner.

In the Data Source tab, you can:

  • Join sheets if there are more than one and they contain overlapping categories.
  • Modify names and data types for each column.
  • Pivot columns, to correct layout.
  • Hide columns you do not anticipate using. This will reduce clutter in the lists of measures and dimensions that appears on the left side in a page tab.
  • Choose to use live data or a data extract. Using an extract can save processing time, especially while you are developing your dashboard.
